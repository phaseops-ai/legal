# Privacy Policy — PhaseOps

**Last updated:** December 16, 2025

This privacy policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices available to people who interact with our website, Meta (Facebook & Instagram) integrations, and automation tools.

---

## 1. Who we are

We design, build, and run AI-powered automations for businesses. This policy applies to our website, integrations with Meta, and any services we deliver to clients.

---

## 2. Information we collect

We collect only what is necessary to deliver and improve services:

**Personal & business contact data:**  
Names, email addresses, phone numbers, company names, and job titles — typically captured from lead forms or client-supplied records.

**Platform and Meta data:**  
Data we access via Meta APIs when enabled by a client (e.g., messages, leads, engagement metrics).

**Technical & usage data:**  
IP addresses, browser/OS, pages visited, and other analytics used to operate and secure services.

**Credentials & metadata:**  
API tokens and system metadata needed to connect and orchestrate services (stored securely).

We follow a data-minimization approach: we do not keep or use information we don’t need for the stated purpose.

---

## 3. How we use information

We use data to:

- Deliver and maintain automations and integrations (lead capture, message routing, CRM sync).
- Improve and secure our systems (debugging, performance monitoring, fraud prevention).
- Communicate with clients and users (service notices, support).
- Provide aggregated, non-identifying analytics to help clients measure performance.

We do **not** sell Meta user data or any personal data derived from Meta APIs.

---

## 4. Automated processing & human review

We use Large Language Models and automation agents to process information and assist with drafting or routing. Important decisions that have legal or similarly significant effects are reviewed or approved by a human before action.

---

## 5. Legal bases for processing

Where applicable, our lawful bases include:

- **Consent** — for marketing and specific Meta interactions when you opt-in.
- **Contract performance** — to deliver services under client agreements.
- **Legitimate interests** — for security, service improvement, and reliable operations, balanced against individual privacy rights.

---

## 6. Third-party services — Meta Platforms and others

We may connect with reputable third parties to provide services, including (but not limited to):

- Meta APIs (for lead capture and messaging)
- Cloud storage and analytics services (e.g., Google services)
- Orchestration platforms (e.g., n8n) and client CRM systems (e.g., Airtable)

Each integration is reviewed for security and compliance. Where relevant, third parties process data under contract and only for permitted purposes.

---

## 7. Meta users: deletion & control

If you interacted with our services via Facebook or Instagram, you can ask us to delete your data:

1. Email a deletion request to our Data Protection Officer at the address below (subject: **"Meta Data Deletion Request"**).
2. You can also remove our app from your Meta account under **Settings → Apps and Websites**.
3. We will confirm deletion once completed.

---

## 8. Data retention and security

We retain personal data only as long as needed for the purpose it was collected or as required by contract or law. When no longer needed, data is deleted or anonymized.

Security practices include encryption in transit and at rest, role-based access controls, and logging/auditing. We enforce multi-factor authentication for staff access where appropriate.

---

## 9. Your privacy rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights such as:

- Access to the personal data we hold about you.
- Correction of inaccurate data.
- Deletion or restriction of processing, subject to legal or contractual obligations.
- Data portability in a common, machine-readable format.
- To object to processing based on legitimate interests.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the information below. You may also file a complaint with your local data protection authority.

---

## 10. International data transfers

We may store or process data in servers located outside your country. When we transfer data internationally, we apply appropriate safeguards (contracts, standard contractual clauses, or equivalent protections).

---

## 11. Children’s privacy

Our services are intended for business users. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 18. If we learn we have collected information from a minor, we will promptly delete it.

---

## 12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy to reflect changes in our services or legal requirements. The **“Last updated”** date above shows when this policy was last revised.
